# Battery Safety — Guide (Lithium / LiPo / Li-Ion / NiMH)

## Common Symptoms (of unsafe battery conditions)
- Battery pack visibly swollen, puffy, or bulging
- Battery feels hot without being in use
- Battery emits hissing, crackling, or popping sounds
- Burning smell or visible smoke from battery
- Battery leaking fluid or white/black residue
- Device extremely hot during charging
- Battery charges very slowly or not at all even with correct charger
- Battery capacity has dropped dramatically in short time

## Likely Causes
- Swollen battery → overcharging, overheating, internal short, end of cycle life, manufacturing defect
- Hot without use → internal short circuit, thermal runaway beginning
- Hissing/crackling → cell venting (controlled venting before thermal runaway) — urgent evacuation needed
- Smoke or fire → thermal runaway — emergency situation
- Leaking → cell seal failed, internal pressure, physical damage
- Extremely hot during charge → charger fault, wrong charger, cell fault
- Slow/no charge → battery has reached end of cycle life, cell below recovery voltage, charger fault
- Rapid capacity loss → too many deep discharge cycles, storage at full charge, heat exposure

## Safe First Checks
- Inspect battery visually for any swelling before charging or use
- Check battery temperature — should be cool to warm during normal use/charge, not hot
- Check charger is correct type and voltage for battery chemistry
- Verify you are not charging at a current exceeding the battery's maximum charge rate
- Check whether battery has been physically impacted or dropped

## Tools Needed
- Non-flammable container with lid (metal tin or ceramic pot) for isolation
- Water supply nearby (for lithium battery fire suppression, NOT standard approach — see Stop Conditions)
- Multimeter (to check cell voltage)
- Fireproof LiPo charging bag (recommended for regular LiPo use)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Before every charge cycle → briefly inspect battery for swelling. If swollen at all → do NOT charge. Proceed to isolation steps.
2. For swollen battery → stop using immediately. Do not charge. Do not compress or pierce. Place in a fireproof container (metal) or LiPo safety bag in an open outdoor area, away from flammables.
3. For battery that feels warm during normal use → this is acceptable. Battery hot to touch → stop use, disconnect, allow to cool in open air before assessing.
4. Check charger voltage and chemistry match. Charging a LiFePO4 cell with a Li-Ion charger, or vice versa, causes overcharge and cell damage.
5. For LiPo batteries → always charge in a LiPo safety bag. Never leave charging unattended. Never charge at above 1C rate without confirming the battery's maximum charge rate specification.
6. For maximum battery lifespan → store at 40–60% charge (3.7–3.8V per cell for Li-Ion/LiPo). Avoid storing fully charged or fully depleted.
7. For battery that won't charge → check cell voltage with multimeter. LiPo/Li-Ion cell below 2.5V may not be recoverable by standard charger. Most chargers won't charge cells below recovery threshold for safety.
8. For NiMH batteries that feel very hot during charge → charger's delta-V detection may have failed. Remove and allow to cool. Use a charger with thermal detection.
9. For battery life extension → avoid full 0–100% cycles. Charge to 80% for daily use if device supports it. Keep battery away from heat (don't leave phone in hot car).
10. Dispose of all lithium batteries through certified battery recycling — not household trash. Damaged batteries: place in metal container with cat litter/sand, seal, and bring to hazardous waste facility.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** immediately if battery produces smoke, hissing, or crackling — move device to open outdoor area, stand back 3+ meters
- **STOP** if thermal runaway begins (rapid heating, smoke, sparks) — evacuate immediately, call fire services
- **STOP** using or charging any swollen lithium battery — thermal runaway risk
- **STOP** if you see battery leaking — do not touch the electrolyte (toxic and caustic), isolate outdoors
- **Never** pierce, cut, crush, or disassemble a lithium battery cell
- **Never** put a lithium battery fire out with a Class A water extinguisher — use large quantities of water ONLY to cool from a safe distance. A LiPo fire is self-oxidizing and cannot be "extinguished" — only cooled
- **Never** place a suspect lithium battery in household trash — fire risk in garbage trucks and waste facilities

## Prevention
- Store lithium batteries at room temperature — heat is the primary cause of degradation
- Never charge batteries on flammable surfaces (sofa, bed, carpet)
- Use manufacturer-supplied or certified chargers only — counterfeit chargers are a leading cause of battery fires
- Inspect lithium batteries for swelling every 3–6 months
- Replace any battery pack that has more than 500 full cycles or shows >20% capacity loss
