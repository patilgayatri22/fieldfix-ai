# Electrical Safety — Guide for Repair Work

## Common Symptoms (of unsafe conditions)
- Burning smell near outlets, switches, or appliances
- Outlet or switch plate warm or hot to touch
- Visible scorch marks or discoloration on outlets or panels
- Circuit breaker trips repeatedly on same circuit
- Lights dim when appliances turn on (voltage fluctuation)
- Sparks or arcing visible when plugging in devices
- Electrical shock felt from appliance casing or outlet
- Buzzing or humming from walls or outlets

## Likely Causes
- Burning smell → overloaded circuit, arcing at loose connection, insulation degrading
- Hot outlet/plate → overloaded outlet, loose wiring behind outlet creating resistance
- Scorch marks → past arcing event — significant fault has occurred
- Repeated breaker trips → overloaded circuit, short circuit, failing appliance, wiring fault
- Lights dim → circuit overloaded, loose neutral wire, utility-side issue
- Sparks on plug-in → normal (small spark OK on initial connection), large sparks = fault
- Shock from casing → appliance ground fault — stop using immediately
- Buzzing from walls → wiring loosened, transformer nearby, or early arcing fault

## Safe First Checks
- For any burning smell → immediately unplug nearby devices and check for heat source
- For repeated breaker trips → do NOT keep resetting without finding cause — count how many items are on that circuit
- For hot outlets → do NOT continue using — unplug all devices on that outlet immediately
- For shock from appliance → unplug immediately — do not touch again until fault is diagnosed
- Non-contact voltage tester: most important safety tool — always verify power is OFF before touching wires

## Tools Needed
- Non-contact voltage tester (essential — every household should own one)
- Outlet tester / circuit analyzer
- Flashlight

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. If you suspect electrical fault → use non-contact voltage tester to confirm power status of circuit before approaching wiring.
2. For overloaded circuit (breaker trips) → add up wattage of all devices on that circuit. Standard circuit handles 1800W (15A×120V) or 2400W (20A×120V). If sum exceeds limit → move some devices to different circuit.
3. For hot outlet → turn off circuit at breaker. Plug in outlet tester to check correct wiring. If outlet tester shows "open ground" or "reversed polarity" → outlet has wiring fault, do not use, call electrician.
4. For appliance causing shock → test by plugging into outlet tester. Do not plug shock-causing appliance back in. The appliance has a ground fault. Repair or replace it.
5. For scorch marks on outlet → turn off circuit at breaker. Do not use outlet. Scorched outlets must be replaced by qualified electrician — past arcing indicates damaged wiring behind the wall.
6. For sparks when plugging in → small spark on initial plug-in is normal (inrush current). Sustained sparking or large spark = fault. Unplug immediately, assess appliance and outlet.
7. Before any wiring work (changing outlets, switches) → turn off the correct circuit breaker. Test with non-contact tester. Test again after toggling breaker. Confirm zero voltage before touching any wire.
8. Never work on wiring alone — have another person present who can call for help if needed.
9. Work with one hand when possible near electrical components — reduces risk of current path across chest.
10. After any electrical repair → test with outlet tester before full use. Have GFCI outlets installed in bathrooms, kitchens, outdoors — these protect against shock.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** all work immediately if non-contact tester indicates voltage is still present — power is not off
- **STOP** and call an electrician if you see burn marks inside an outlet or panel, melted wire insulation, or signs of past fire
- **STOP** if circuit breaker is hot or has a burning smell — call electrician immediately, do not reset
- **STOP** if someone is in contact with live electrical current — do NOT touch them — cut power at breaker first, then assist
- **Never** work on the main electrical panel (breaker box) — the main lugs remain live even with the main breaker off
- **Never** bypass a circuit breaker or fuse with wire — this removes fire protection
- **Never** use electrical equipment near water without GFCI protection

## Prevention
- Install GFCI outlets in all wet areas (kitchen, bathroom, garage, outdoors)
- Install AFCI breakers on bedroom and living area circuits — prevents arc-fault fires
- Have electrical panel inspected by licensed electrician every 10 years
- Do not overload power strips or extension cords — use one device per outlet for high-draw appliances
- Replace any outlet or switch with scorch marks or that feels warm — even if still functional
