# Emergency Procedures — Guide

## Common Emergency Scenarios
- Suspected gas leak (smell of sulfur / rotten eggs)
- Electrical fire (sparks, burning wiring, smoke from outlet/panel)
- Lithium battery fire or thermal runaway
- Flooding (water entering home or workspace)
- Carbon monoxide alarm activation
- Electric shock to a person
- Chemical spill in workspace
- Structural collapse risk (falling ceiling, cracked wall)

## Safe First Checks

> In any emergency: protect yourself first. You cannot help others if you are injured.

- Assess situation from a safe distance before approaching
- Never put yourself in danger to save equipment or property
- Know where your home's main shutoffs are (water, gas, electricity) before an emergency happens
- Keep emergency numbers posted visibly: fire, police, poison control, gas company

## Tools Needed
- Smoke and CO detector (installed and tested monthly)
- Class ABC fire extinguisher
- First aid kit (stocked and current)
- Flashlight with fresh batteries
- Emergency phone (charged)

## Step-by-Step Guidance by Scenario

### Gas Leak
1. Do NOT operate any electrical switch, light, or appliance — sparks can ignite gas.
2. Do NOT use mobile phone inside the building.
3. Evacuate everyone immediately. Leave door open as you exit.
4. Call gas emergency number from outside or neighbor's phone.
5. Do not re-enter until gas company confirms it is safe.

### Electrical Fire
1. Turn off power at the breaker immediately if safe to do so — do not reach past smoke or flames.
2. Use a Class C or Class ABC fire extinguisher only — NEVER use water on electrical fire.
3. If fire is not immediately controllable → evacuate, close doors, call fire services.
4. Do not re-enter building after evacuating — wait for fire services.

### Lithium Battery Fire / Thermal Runaway
1. Do not attempt to extinguish — lithium battery fires are self-oxidizing and cannot be put out conventionally.
2. If outdoors or easily moved: place burning battery on non-flammable surface outside and apply large quantities of water to cool (not extinguish).
3. If indoors and spreading: evacuate immediately, call fire services.
4. Stand at least 3 meters back — battery can vent and eject burning material.

### Carbon Monoxide Alarm
1. Evacuate immediately — CO is odorless and acts quickly.
2. Do not stop to collect belongings.
3. Call emergency services from outside.
4. Do not re-enter until authorities clear the building.
5. Identify and repair CO source (furnace, generator, appliance) before returning.

### Electric Shock to a Person
1. Do NOT touch the person if they are still in contact with the electrical source — you will also be shocked.
2. Turn off power at the breaker or unplug source immediately.
3. Only then approach and assess the person.
4. Call emergency services (911). Even if person appears unharmed — internal injuries from electrical shock are not always visible.
5. If person is unresponsive and not breathing → begin CPR if trained.

### Flooding / Water Intrusion
1. Turn off electricity to the affected area at the breaker — never stand in water near electrical items.
2. Turn off main water supply if source is a burst pipe.
3. Do not use electrical appliances that have been submerged — even after drying, they may have internal damage.
4. Document damage with photos for insurance before cleaning up.
5. Ventilate and begin drying within 24 hours to prevent mold — fans and dehumidifiers.

### Chemical Spill
1. Remove any clothing contaminated with chemical.
2. Flush affected skin with running water for 15 minutes.
3. For eye contact → flush with running water for 15 minutes — seek medical attention.
4. Ventilate area immediately.
5. Refer to product's Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for specific instructions.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** — In all emergencies: protect human life first, property second
- **STOP** re-entering any building after evacuation until declared safe by emergency services
- **STOP** attempting to save equipment during an active fire or gas leak
- **STOP** touching a person receiving electric shock until power is confirmed off
- **NEVER** assume a CO or smoke alarm is false — treat every activation as real

## Prevention
- Know your home's main water, gas, and electrical shutoff locations — practice finding them in the dark
- Install smoke detectors on every floor and CO detectors near sleeping areas
- Test all detectors monthly
- Keep a Class ABC fire extinguisher accessible in kitchen and any workshop
- Review emergency procedures with all household members annually
