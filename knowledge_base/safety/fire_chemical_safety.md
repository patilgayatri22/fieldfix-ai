# Fire & Chemical Safety — Guide for Repair Work

## Common Symptoms (of unsafe conditions)
- Smell of smoke with no visible source
- Burning smell from electronics or wiring
- Visible sparks from an appliance or outlet
- Solvent or chemical fumes stronger than expected in workspace
- Flammable liquid has spilled
- Contact cleaner or aerosol sprayed near a heat source
- Visible open flame or glowing ember near work area
- Smoke rising from electronics during operation

## Likely Causes
- Smoke with no source → electrical fire inside wall, smoldering insulation, hidden hot spot
- Burning smell from electronics → component failure, overloaded wiring, solder overheated
- Sparks from outlet/appliance → arcing at loose connection, short circuit
- Strong fumes → solvent evaporating in enclosed space, product off-gassing
- Spilled flammable liquid → immediate ignition risk if heat source present
- Aerosol near heat → propellant in many aerosols is flammable
- Smoke from electronics → component burning, thermal event in progress

## Safe First Checks
- Identify the smell source before assuming false alarm — smell of burning = take seriously
- Ensure workspace has ventilation (open window or fan) when using any chemical spray or solvent
- Read warning label on ALL spray products before use — many contain flammable propellants
- Check work area for ignition sources (soldering iron, open flame, hot surfaces) before using sprays
- Know location of fire extinguisher before starting repair work

## Tools Needed
- Class C fire extinguisher (rated for electrical fires) or Class ABC extinguisher
- Safety goggles
- Nitrile gloves (chemical-resistant)
- Good ventilation or respirator rated for organic vapors (for solvent work)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. When burning smell is detected → stop work immediately. Identify source. If source not found quickly → evacuate area and call fire services. Do not search in the dark.
2. For electrical burning smell → see electrical_safety.md. Turn off circuit at breaker. Do not operate appliance again until fault found.
3. When using aerosol contact cleaner, flux remover, or circuit board cleaner → ensure no open flames or hot solder iron nearby. Turn off soldering iron and allow 1 minute to cool before spraying.
4. For spilled flammable solvent (isopropyl alcohol, acetone, contact cleaner) → immediately remove all ignition sources. Ventilate area. Wipe up with absorbent cloth. Allow area to air out for 5 minutes before resuming work.
5. For strong chemical fumes (from adhesives, solvents, spray paint) → work outdoors or with strong ventilation. Vapors from many solvents are heavier than air and accumulate at floor level.
6. For fire from small electronics (battery or component fire) → disconnect power source if safe to do so. Small fires — use Class C or ABC extinguisher from a distance. Lithium battery fires → large quantities of water to cool (not extinguish) from safe distance. Evacuate if not controllable.
7. For soldering in enclosed spaces → fumes from flux and solder contain harmful particulates and aldehydes. Use a fume extractor or direct a fan to blow fumes away from your face.
8. For chemical contact with skin → rinse with running water for 15 minutes. Check Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for the specific chemical for further guidance.
9. For eye contact with chemicals → flush with clean running water for 15 minutes. Seek medical attention immediately.
10. Never pour chemical waste (solvents, lubricants, cleaning agents) down a drain — use hazardous waste disposal or allow to evaporate in ventilated area.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** all work and evacuate if fire is not immediately controllable with a fire extinguisher
- **STOP** if smoke fills a room — evacuate, close doors, call emergency services
- **STOP** using aerosols near any heat source, open flame, or soldering iron
- **STOP** if you smell gas (rotten egg odor) — evacuate immediately, do not operate electrical switches, call gas emergency line from outside
- **Never** use water on an electrical fire — use a Class C or ABC extinguisher
- **Never** re-enter a smoke-filled room without self-contained breathing equipment
- **Never** inhale fumes from burning electronics, plastics, or solder — toxic

## Prevention
- Always have a Class ABC or Class C fire extinguisher accessible in any repair workspace
- Store flammable liquids in clearly labeled, sealed containers away from heat sources
- Work in ventilated spaces when using any chemical spray or solvent
- Keep a clear path to exits from any repair workspace
- Learn the PASS technique for fire extinguisher use: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep
