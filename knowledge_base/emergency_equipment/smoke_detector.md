# Smoke Detector — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Intermittent beeping (chirping) — one beep every 30–60 seconds
- Continuous alarm with no visible smoke
- Alarm doesn't sound during test button press
- Alarm sounds when cooking or showering
- Red LED not blinking in normal operation
- Detector is more than 10 years old

## Likely Causes
- Chirping every 30–60 seconds → low battery (most common cause)
- False alarms (no smoke) → cooking steam/smoke, shower steam, insects inside unit, dust buildup, near AC/heating vent
- No alarm on test → dead battery, detector failed, wired unit lost AC power
- Cooking/shower triggers → detector placed too close to kitchen or bathroom
- Red LED not blinking → battery dead, unit has failed
- 10+ year old detector → sensor element degraded — replace regardless of apparent function

## Safe First Checks
- Check battery: remove detector from ceiling, open battery compartment, inspect and replace battery
- Use test button: press and hold — alarm should sound within 5 seconds
- Check production date label on back of detector — over 10 years = replace
- Inspect detector for dust, insects, or visible contamination on sensor vents

## Tools Needed
- Replacement 9V battery or AA batteries (check unit type)
- Step stool or ladder
- Soft brush or compressed air can (for cleaning)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. If chirping → replace battery immediately. Remove detector from mount. Open battery compartment. Install new battery with correct polarity. Remount and test.
2. After battery replacement, press test button — alarm should sound. If not → detector has failed, replace unit.
3. For false alarms from cooking → check detector placement. Should be minimum 3 meters from kitchen. Relocate if too close.
4. For false alarms from shower steam → check if within 3 meters of bathroom. Ensure bathroom door kept closed when showering. Relocate detector if needed.
5. For dust/insect false alarms → press test button, then carefully vacuum detector vents with brush attachment. Use compressed air to blow out debris from sensing chamber vents.
6. For hardwired detector chirping even with good 9V battery → chirp may indicate AC power loss. Check circuit breaker for that area of home.
7. For hardwired interconnected alarm triggering all units → one unit in system is triggering. Identify which unit by presence of smoke/steam near it. Reset system after clearing cause.
8. Check production date on back of every detector. If over 10 years → replace, regardless of test pass.
9. If test button does not trigger alarm and battery is fresh → detector has reached end of life. Replace immediately.
10. Note: photoelectric detectors are better for smoldering fires; ionization detectors are better for fast flaming fires. For best protection, use both types.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** disabling or removing the battery from a smoke detector — this removes life safety protection
- **STOP** ignoring persistent or repeated false alarms — investigate the cause, do not just silence and ignore
- If alarm sounds and source of smoke is not immediately obvious → treat as real fire, evacuate, call emergency services
- Do not spray cleaners, paint, or aerosols near detectors

## Prevention
- Test smoke detectors monthly using the test button
- Replace battery annually (or when low-battery chirp begins)
- Replace entire detector every 10 years — sensor cells degrade
- Vacuum detector vents annually to prevent dust buildup triggering false alarms
- Install at least one detector per floor and outside every sleeping area
