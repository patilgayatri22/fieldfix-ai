# Flashlight — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Flashlight completely dead — no light
- Dim light even with fresh batteries
- Flickering or intermittent light
- Light works briefly then cuts out
- Only high mode works, low/strobe modes don't
- Flashlight gets hot very quickly
- Clicking sound from driver but no light
- Lens or reflector has visible condensation inside

## Likely Causes
- Completely dead → dead batteries, corroded contacts, loose tailcap or head, broken switch
- Dim light → weak/old batteries, corroded contacts increasing resistance, LED dying
- Flickering → loose connection (common at tailcap spring), contact corrosion, failing switch
- Cuts out after short use → thermal protection activating (LED overheating), weak battery collapses under load
- Modes not working → driver malfunction, mode memory issue, wrong click pattern
- Gets hot quickly → high output mode, inadequate heatsinking (normal for high-lumen lights), blocked ventilation
- Driver clicking → driver board fault, short circuit on LED side
- Condensation inside → seal failed, temperature cycling, exposed to rain

## Safe First Checks
- Check battery polarity — insert correctly per markings
- Measure battery voltage: AA full = 1.5V, 18650 full = 4.2V, minimum = 3.0V
- Unscrew and re-tighten tailcap and head — many flashlights fail from loose threads
- Inspect battery tube contacts for white/blue corrosion crust
- Check O-rings on tailcap and head — should be intact and lightly lubricated

## Tools Needed
- Multimeter
- Contact cleaner or isopropyl alcohol
- Cotton swabs
- White vinegar (alkaline leak cleanup)
- Flashlight body grease or silicone O-ring lubricant (thin smear)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Open flashlight. Remove batteries. Measure battery voltage — alkaline AA dead < 1.1V, 18650 dead < 3.0V.
2. Inspect battery tube interior. White/blue residue = battery leakage. Clean with white vinegar on swab (neutralizes alkaline). Then clean with isopropyl alcohol.
3. Check positive contact (brass button or spring at head end) and negative contact (spring at tailcap). Press springs — should spring back firmly. Blackened = oxidized.
4. Clean all contacts with isopropyl alcohol swab. For stubborn oxidation, use pencil eraser then isopropyl.
5. Reassemble with fresh batteries. Ensure tailcap is fully tightened.
6. If flickering → slightly unscrew tailcap by 1/8 turn then retighten — reseats the contact. If flicker stops then returns → tailcap spring contact is weak.
7. For intermittent cutout → tap tailcap firmly. If light comes on → tailcap spring contact. Check spring height and tension. Carefully stretch spring if compressed flat.
8. For modes not working → perform hard reset: hold power button 5–10 seconds, or remove and reinsert battery. Different driver ICs have different reset methods.
9. For condensation inside → unscrew completely, place in dry warm location for 24–48 hours. Check O-ring condition and replace if cracked/flat.
10. If light still dead after all above → LED or driver board failed. Beyond field repair — contact manufacturer or replace flashlight.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if flashlight is extremely hot (can't hold) — thermal protection should trip; if it doesn't, power off immediately
- **STOP** if batteries are swollen, hot, or leaking — remove carefully, do not use
- **STOP** if any burning smell — potential electrical fault or battery failure
- Never use rechargeable lithium batteries not rated for the flashlight's current draw

## Prevention
- Remove batteries for storage longer than 1 month
- Lightly grease O-rings with silicone grease annually to maintain water resistance
- Inspect battery contacts every 6 months even without visible issues
- Store flashlight with fresh batteries in an accessible emergency location
- Test flashlight monthly if kept for emergency use
