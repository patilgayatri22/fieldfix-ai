# Portable Generator — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Generator won't start — engine doesn't crank or cranks but won't fire
- Engine starts then stalls after a few seconds
- Engine runs but no electrical output
- Output voltage fluctuates or is incorrect
- Generator surges — engine RPM oscillates up and down
- Engine runs roughly or backfires
- Generator producing black smoke
- Very difficult to pull start cord

## Likely Causes
- Won't start → no fuel, stale fuel, choke position wrong, spark plug fouled/dead, low oil shutdown active
- Starts then stalls → choke opened too early, carburetor dirty/clogged, air filter blocked
- No electrical output → circuit breaker tripped, AVR (automatic voltage regulator) fault, brushes worn (brushed alternator)
- Voltage fluctuates → engine governor not maintaining RPM, AVR fault, overload
- Surging → dirty carburetor, incorrect idle speed, governor spring stretched
- Rough running/backfire → stale fuel, dirty carburetor, incorrect ignition timing
- Black smoke → rich fuel mixture, choke stuck closed, dirty air filter
- Hard to pull → engine hydrolocked (oil in cylinder), compression release valve fault

## Safe First Checks
- Check fuel level — fill with fresh fuel if empty or fuel is over 30 days old
- Check engine oil level via dipstick — most generators have low-oil shutdown; add oil if low
- Check circuit breaker/overload switch on generator's output panel — reset if tripped
- Check choke position — full closed for cold start, open after engine warms (30–60 seconds)
- Check air filter — remove and inspect, replace if dirty or clogged

## Tools Needed
- Fresh fuel (stabilizer-treated)
- Engine oil (check generator spec for grade, typically 10W-30 or SAE 30)
- Spark plug wrench
- Replacement spark plug (match OEM spec)
- Carburetor cleaner spray

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Check fuel and oil levels before any other step. Both must be adequate to start.
2. Prime fuel if equipped (primer bulb — press 3–5 times until resistance felt).
3. Set choke to CLOSED (cold start). Pull starter cord firmly — do not yank repeatedly without choke.
4. Once engine fires and runs roughly → slowly open choke to half, then full over 30–60 seconds.
5. If engine won't fire after 5 pulls → check spark plug. Remove plug with spark plug wrench. Inspect: black sooty = rich, white/grey = correct, wet with fuel = flooded.
6. For wet/flooded spark plug → let sit for 5 minutes with plug removed to vent. Reinstall dry plug and retry with choke OPEN.
7. For sooty/fouled plug → clean electrode with wire brush or replace. Spark plugs are cheap — replacement is safest.
8. If engine runs but surges → carburetor is likely dirty. Spray carburetor cleaner into air intake while engine runs. If surging improves → carburetor cleaning needed. Do not disassemble carburetor without prior experience.
9. For no electrical output → reset circuit breaker on output panel. Reduce load (disconnect appliances). If breaker resets but output absent → AVR or alternator fault — requires professional service.
10. For black smoke → open choke fully. Remove and inspect air filter. If air filter clean and smoke continues → carburetor adjustment needed — professional service.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** and do not operate generator indoors, in garages, or near open windows — carbon monoxide is odorless and lethal
- **STOP** if you smell fuel leaking or see fuel dripping near exhaust — fire hazard
- **STOP** if engine produces white smoke (steam) — possible internal damage
- **STOP** if engine knocks loudly — low oil or mechanical damage — shut off immediately
- Never refuel while engine is running or hot
- Never connect generator to home wiring without proper transfer switch — risk to utility workers

## Prevention
- Run generator under load for 30 minutes every 3 months if in storage
- Add fuel stabilizer to any fuel stored more than 30 days
- Change engine oil per manufacturer schedule (typically 20–50 hours)
- Replace spark plug annually or per manufacturer interval
- Keep air filter clean — inspect before each extended use
