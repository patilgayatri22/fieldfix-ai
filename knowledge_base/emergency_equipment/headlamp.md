# Headlamp — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Headlamp won't turn on at all
- Only red mode or only white mode works
- Light is very dim with fresh batteries
- Headlamp stuck in one mode — can't cycle modes
- Headlamp turns on briefly then shuts off
- Flickers when tilting or moving head
- Strap elastic stretched — won't stay on head
- Condensation visible inside lens

## Likely Causes
- Won't turn on → dead batteries, corroded contacts, broken power button
- Only one LED group works → wiring break to one LED, driver fault
- Dim with fresh batteries → battery contact oxidation, wrong battery type (1.2V NiMH vs 1.5V alkaline)
- Stuck in mode → button switch stuck, driver stuck in mode-memory
- Shuts off quickly → thermal protection, battery collapses under high-drain
- Flickers on movement → loose battery contact, internal wire loose at battery compartment
- Strap stretched → normal wear, replace strap or add velcro extender
- Condensation → O-ring seal failed, worn or cracked, submerged beyond rated depth

## Safe First Checks
- Measure battery voltage before assuming headlamp is dead
- Check battery orientation — correct polarity per markings
- Clean battery contacts with pencil eraser
- For headlamps with separate battery pack: check cable connection at both ends
- Check mode button — press firmly multiple times to ensure not mechanically stuck

## Tools Needed
- Multimeter
- Isopropyl alcohol and cotton swabs
- Pencil eraser
- Small Phillips screwdriver

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Remove batteries. Measure voltage. Replace if AA below 1.2V or AAA below 1.1V.
2. Inspect battery compartment contacts — clean with isopropyl alcohol swab. Clean any corrosion with pencil eraser.
3. Reinstall batteries with correct polarity. Test.
4. If dim with NiMH rechargeable → nominal NiMH voltage is 1.2V vs 1.5V alkaline. Many headlamps show dim warning with NiMH even when charged. This is normal — NiMH still provides adequate runtime.
5. If stuck in mode → remove batteries, wait 30 seconds, reinstall. Attempt mode cycling with fresh button presses.
6. If only one LED group works → check if there's a separate dedicated switch for each mode (some models have two buttons). If single-button model, driver or wiring fault — beyond field repair.
7. For flickering on movement → apply gentle pressure to battery compartment lid while on. If flicker stops → lid contact is loose. Check contact spring tension and tighten compartment lid.
8. For headlamp turning off after short use → try new alkaline batteries — thermal shutoff triggers earlier on weak batteries as they sag under load.
9. For condensation → dry headlamp in warm place for 24 hours. Check O-ring seal around lens. Replace if cracked.
10. For stretched strap → thread strap through a velcro extension or tie a knot in strap behind adjustment slider to shorten effective length.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if headlamp battery compartment shows swollen or leaking batteries — remove carefully
- **STOP** if headlamp is hot to touch through casing — potential battery fault
- Do not attempt to open LED driver module — no user-serviceable parts inside

## Prevention
- Replace batteries when brightness noticeably decreases, not when completely dead
- Remove batteries for storage over 2 months
- Check O-ring condition annually — lightly grease with silicone grease
- After water exposure, dry fully before storing (even rated waterproof headlamps can trap moisture inside)
- Test headlamp monthly if kept in emergency kit
