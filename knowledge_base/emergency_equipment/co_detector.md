# Carbon Monoxide (CO) Detector — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Intermittent chirping (every 30–60 seconds)
- Alarm sounds but no obvious CO source present
- Red LED flashing or solid
- Display shows "END" or "ERR"
- Detector is over 5–7 years old
- Alarm won't reset after pressing silence button

## Likely Causes
- Chirping → low battery or end-of-life signal from sensor
- False alarm → CO source actually present (check — do not assume false), detector near gas appliances, cleaning solvents
- "END" or "ERR" on display → sensor has reached end of life (CO sensors last 5–7 years)
- Alarm won't reset → CO levels in air still elevated, sensor has failed in alarm state
- Over 5–7 years old → sensor element chemically depleted — replace regardless

## Safe First Checks

> **IMPORTANT:** If CO alarm is sounding continuously — do not troubleshoot first. Evacuate immediately and call emergency services. Re-enter only when declared safe.

- Pressing the test/silence button: silences alarm temporarily but does NOT confirm no CO present
- Check production date on back label — CO sensor lifespan is 5–7 years
- Assess potential CO sources: gas furnace, water heater, fireplace, attached garage, gas stove, generator
- Ventilate space (open windows and doors) before re-entering to investigate

## Tools Needed
- Replacement 9V battery or AA batteries (check unit type)
- Replacement CO detector (if unit is at end of life)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. If alarm is sounding → evacuate all people and pets immediately. Call 911 / emergency services from outside.
2. Do NOT re-enter until authorities confirm CO levels are safe.
3. If alarm was intermittent chirping and CO source has been ruled out → check battery first. Replace battery.
4. After battery replacement, press test button — alarm should sound briefly. If "END" or sensor error appears → detector sensor is expired, replace unit.
5. Check production date sticker on back. If older than 5–7 years (varies by brand) → replace detector now.
6. After replacing unit → test new unit immediately. Confirm alarm sounds on test.
7. For detector placed near gas appliances and triggering → check appliance is properly maintained and vented. CO detectors should not be placed directly above cooktops — place at breathing height (1.5m) at least 3 meters from fuel-burning appliances.
8. If alarm returns after resetting → do not attempt to silence and leave. CO may be present. Evacuate and call gas company.
9. For hardwired units → if chirping continues with good battery → check AC power supply. Loss of AC causes chirp on some models.
10. Never use CO detector as substitute for regular appliance maintenance — annual servicing of heating appliances by qualified technician is essential.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** re-entering any space where CO alarm is/was sounding until declared safe by emergency services
- **STOP** assuming a CO alarm is "false" without verified investigation — CO is odorless and colorless
- **STOP** disabling or removing batteries from CO detector — this removes critical life safety protection
- Never operate generators, grills, or camp stoves indoors or in attached garages

## Prevention
- Replace CO detector every 5–7 years — electrochemical sensors degrade and cannot be recalibrated by user
- Test CO detector monthly using the test button
- Have all fuel-burning appliances (furnace, water heater, fireplace) serviced annually by a qualified technician
- Install CO detector within 3 meters of every sleeping area
- Never run gasoline engines (generators, cars, lawnmowers) in garages — even with door open
