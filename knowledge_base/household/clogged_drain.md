# Clogged Drain — Repair Guide (Sink / Shower / Bathtub)

## Common Symptoms
- Water drains very slowly (sink or shower)
- Water pools completely and doesn't drain
- Gurgling sounds from drain after water drains
- Bad smell coming from drain
- Multiple drains slow at same time
- Toilet bubbles when sink drains nearby

## Likely Causes
- Slow sink drain → hair/soap buildup in stopper mechanism or P-trap
- Slow shower/tub → hair clump at drain cover, soap scum accumulation
- Complete blockage → accumulated hair/grease plug, foreign object
- Gurgling sounds → partial blockage slowing airflow in drain vent, vent pipe issue
- Bad smell → organic matter decomposing in drain, dry P-trap (rarely used sink)
- Multiple drains slow simultaneously → main drain line blockage — further investigation needed
- Toilet bubbles when sink drains → shared vent pipe obstruction

## Safe First Checks
- Remove and inspect drain stopper/cover — most bathroom sink stoppers lift out or unscrew
- Look down drain with a flashlight — visible hair clump near surface is easiest fix
- Check if issue is just this drain or multiple drains in home
- Test toilet: if it flushes without gurgling, the clog is likely local (not in main line)

## Tools Needed
- Drain snake or hair clog remover tool (plastic barbed strip)
- Bucket
- Needle-nose pliers
- Baking soda and white vinegar (mild cleaning)
- Rubber gloves
- Cup plunger (for sinks) — NOT a flange plunger (that's for toilets)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. For bathroom sink → pull out the drain stopper (usually lifts up then pull, or unscrew). Clean off soap scum and hair. Rinse and reinstall. Test.
2. For shower/tub → remove drain cover (usually 2 screws or snaps off). Use a flashlight to look inside. Use a barbed plastic hair-clog tool (drain snake) or needle-nose pliers to pull out hair clumps.
3. For sink with pivot rod (stopper connected to pop-up mechanism) → under sink, find horizontal pivot rod going into drainpipe. Unclip the retaining clip, pull out pivot rod. Stopper now lifts out. Clean stopper and the drain opening thoroughly.
4. After removing visible hair/debris → test drain. If still slow, proceed to next step.
5. Pour boiling water down drain in 2–3 stages (30-second intervals). Often dissolves soap scum accumulation.
6. If still slow → baking soda treatment: pour 1/2 cup baking soda into drain. Follow with 1/2 cup white vinegar. Let fizz for 15 minutes. Flush with hot water.
7. For stubborn clog → use a plunger. For sink: block overflow hole with wet cloth, then plunge with cup plunger. Create full seal over drain. Push down firmly and pull up sharply, 10 times. Test drain.
8. For complete blockage → use a manual drain snake (auger). Insert snake into drain, turn handle clockwise while pushing forward. When resistance felt, continue rotating to break up or hook clog. Pull out slowly.
9. For bad smell with no clog → pour 500ml of water down rarely-used drains to refill the P-trap (curved pipe under sink that holds water to block sewer gases). Leave taps running 30 seconds.
10. If multiple drains slow simultaneously → this is likely a main drain line issue. Do not use chemical drain cleaners. Call a plumber — main line blockage requires professional snaking or jetting.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if water is backing up from multiple drains simultaneously — possible main line blockage or sewage backup — call plumber
- **STOP** using chemical drain cleaners (Drano, etc.) if you have PVC pipes under 10 years old — some formulas damage plastic
- **STOP** if drain is completely blocked and you don't have a snake — don't pour more water in; it will overflow
- Do not attempt to disassemble P-trap pipes without bucket ready — water will spill immediately

## Prevention
- Install mesh drain hair catchers in all showers and sinks — removes most clogs before they form
- Flush drains with hot water for 30 seconds monthly
- Clean bathroom sink stoppers every 2–3 months
- Never pour cooking grease down kitchen sink — dispose in trash
- Run cold water for 15 seconds after using garbage disposal
