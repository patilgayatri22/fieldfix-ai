# Door Hinge — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Door squeaks or creaks when opening/closing
- Door sags — bottom scrapes floor or doesn't close fully
- Door rubs on frame on one side
- Hinge screws won't tighten — spin freely in hole
- Door won't stay open or won't stay closed
- Hinge is visibly bent or cracked
- Gap between door and frame is uneven

## Likely Causes
- Squeaking → dry hinge pin, no lubrication, rust on pin
- Door sag → loose hinge screws (especially top hinge), stripped screw holes
- Rubbing on frame → door sag, swollen door (humidity), hinge leaves not flush with mortise
- Screws spinning → wood fibers in screw hole stripped, hole too large
- Won't stay position → hinge spring tension wrong (self-closing hinge), door not balanced
- Bent hinge → heavy door, impact, long-term overload
- Uneven gap → door has warped, hinge has shifted, frame has settled

## Safe First Checks
- Open door to 90°. Check if all hinge screws are tight — try to wiggle each screw by hand
- Look at hinge knuckles for rust or debris
- Check if door bottom clears floor throughout full swing
- Observe where door makes contact with frame (top/bottom/latch side) to locate the problem hinge

## Tools Needed
- Phillips and flathead screwdrivers
- Hammer
- Toothpicks and wood glue (for stripped holes)
- Longer screws (3-inch wood screws)
- Petroleum jelly, WD-40, or 3-in-1 oil (hinge lubrication)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. For squeaking → apply one drop of petroleum jelly or 3-in-1 oil to the hinge pin. Open and close door 10 times to work lubricant in. Wipe excess.
2. To lubricate hinge pin without removing → tap pin upward from below with screwdriver and hammer until top is exposed ~1cm. Apply lubricant to pin. Tap back down.
3. For loose screws → tighten all screws first. If screw still spins → hole is stripped.
4. For stripped screw hole (screw spins, won't tighten) → remove screw. Dip 2–3 wooden toothpicks in wood glue. Insert into hole, break off flush. Allow 20 minutes to dry. Reinstall screw — toothpicks give threads something to grip.
5. For severely stripped holes → use longer 3-inch screws that reach into door frame stud. This also greatly strengthens the hinge attachment.
6. For sagging door (bottom scraping floor) → tighten top hinge first — it carries most of the door weight. If still sagging after tightening → stripped hole, use toothpick/glue method or longer screws.
7. For door rubbing on latch-side frame → this usually means top hinge is loose. Tighten top hinge fully. If door still rubs, place thin cardboard shim behind bottom hinge leaf to adjust angle.
8. For bent hinge → if visible bend is slight (under 3mm), attempt to tap back with hammer and block. If bent more severely → replace hinge. Match size and screw spacing exactly.
9. For hinge leaves not sitting flush in mortise → check if hinge is the correct thickness for the mortise depth. A proud hinge causes the door to rub the stop.
10. For uneven gap after all hinge work → door may have warped. Humid climates cause wood expansion — often resolves seasonally. Persistent warp requires planing or door replacement.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if hinge is cracked through — weight of door may cause hinge to fail suddenly
- **STOP** attempting to straighten a severely bent hinge while under door weight — support door first
- Do not ignore hinge failures on fire-rated doors — replace with proper fire-rated hardware

## Prevention
- Lubricate all door hinges annually — one drop of oil per hinge
- Inspect hinge screws every few years — tighten any that have worked loose
- Use 3-inch screws in at least the top hole of each hinge for maximum strength
- In humid climates, paint or seal door edges to reduce moisture absorption and warping
