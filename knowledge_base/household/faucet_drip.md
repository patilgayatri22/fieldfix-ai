# Faucet / Tap — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Faucet drips from spout when fully off
- Water drips from base of handle
- Low water pressure from this faucet only
- Handle stiff or hard to turn
- Faucet makes squealing noise when turned
- Running toilet — water constantly running into bowl
- Toilet handle must be held down to flush fully
- Banging noise in pipes when faucet turned off (water hammer)

## Likely Causes
- Dripping from spout → worn washer/O-ring (compression faucet), worn cartridge (cartridge faucet), worn ceramic disc
- Drip from handle base → O-ring around cartridge worn
- Low pressure at one faucet → aerator clogged with mineral deposits
- Stiff handle → mineral buildup on cartridge, cartridge needs replacement
- Squealing → rubber washer deteriorated, water speed too high
- Running toilet → flapper valve worn, float set too high, fill valve worn
- Toilet handle stuck down → chain too short, handle mechanism stuck
- Water hammer → no air chamber in pipes, pipes not secured (normal maintenance issue)

## Safe First Checks
- Identify faucet type: compression (two separate hot/cold handles), ball (single round handle), cartridge (single lever), ceramic disc (single lever, wider body)
- Turn off water supply valves under sink before any disassembly
- Check aerator (screen at faucet tip) — unscrew by hand and inspect for scale buildup
- For toilet: lift tank lid, observe water level and flapper seating after flush

## Tools Needed
- Adjustable wrench or pliers (with cloth to protect finish)
- Flathead and Phillips screwdrivers
- Replacement washer set or replacement cartridge (match brand/model)
- Replacement flapper (match toilet model)
- Plumber's grease (silicone grease)
- White vinegar (mineral deposit removal)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Turn off water supply valve(s) under sink. Turn on faucet to release pressure. Confirm water stops.
2. For clogged aerator → unscrew aerator from faucet tip (turn counterclockwise). Soak in white vinegar for 30 minutes. Rinse and reinstall. This fixes most single-faucet low pressure issues.
3. For dripping spout (compression faucet) → remove handle (screw under decorative cap). Unscrew packing nut with wrench. Pull out stem. Inspect rubber washer at bottom — flat or cracked = replace. Replace washer, reassemble.
4. For dripping spout (cartridge faucet) → remove handle by removing set screw (often under cap). Pull out cartridge (straight up). Take old cartridge to hardware store to match. Install new cartridge with correct orientation. Reassemble.
5. For leaking around handle base → O-ring on cartridge worn. Replace cartridge (includes new O-rings) or replace O-ring only if cartridge is otherwise good.
6. For stiff handle → apply plumber's grease to cartridge before reinstalling. If cartridge is old and mineral-encrusted, replacement is more reliable than cleaning.
7. For running toilet → lift tank lid. Observe flapper (rubber disc at bottom of tank). Pour food dye in tank. If dye appears in bowl without flushing → flapper is not sealing. Replace flapper. Match to toilet model.
8. For toilet float too high → adjust float height so water level is 2–3cm below overflow tube. Most floats have an adjustment screw or clip on float arm.
9. If water continues running after flapper replacement → fill valve worn. Fill valve replacement is a slightly larger job — the valve unscrews from tank bottom. Replacement kits are widely available.
10. For water hammer (banging pipes) → this is a pipe pressure issue, not faucet fault. Slow down how quickly you close faucets. If persistent → plumber should install water hammer arrestor — beyond basic DIY.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if you see water spraying from any pipe joint after turning supply back on — retighten or call plumber
- **STOP** if supply valve is corroded or won't turn — do not force, call plumber
- **STOP** if there is visible mold or rot in cabinet under sink — leak has been ongoing, assess extent before proceeding
- Do not work on pipes connected to mains supply pressure without shutting off water — do not attempt any pipe cutting or joining

## Prevention
- Clean aerators annually in hard water areas — mineral buildup reduces flow over time
- Replace rubber washers and O-rings at first sign of dripping — early repair is much simpler than a flooded cabinet
- Test toilet flapper annually: add food dye to tank, wait 10 minutes without flushing — color in bowl = flapper leaking
- Know location of home's main water shutoff valve before any plumbing work
