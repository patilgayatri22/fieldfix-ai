# Wobbly Furniture — Repair Guide (Chairs / Tables / Shelves / Cabinets)

## Common Symptoms
- Chair rocks on all four legs — one leg shorter than others
- Chair joints feel loose — creaking or flexing when sat on
- Table wobbles even on flat floor
- Shelf sags in the middle under load
- Cabinet door hangs crooked or won't close
- Drawer is stiff, won't slide, or falls out
- Wooden joints are visibly separated or gaping
- Leg has snapped or is cracked

## Likely Causes
- One shorter leg → worn foot pad, leg unscrewed partially, uneven floor
- Loose joints → dried-out or failed wood glue, dowel joints shrunken, connector bolts loose
- Table wobble → same as chair + often adjustable foot pads (check if adjustable)
- Shelf sag → shelf material too thin for span, no center support, heavy overloading
- Crooked cabinet door → hinge adjustment needed, screws loose, door has warped
- Stiff drawer → swollen wood, runner dirty or damaged, drawer bottom warped
- Visible joint gap → needs re-gluing
- Cracked/snapped leg → structural repair needed

## Safe First Checks
- Place furniture on flat known-level floor — diagnose on carpet only as last resort
- Rock furniture gently and locate the specific movement source (joint, leg, frame)
- Check all visible connector bolts, cam locks (flat furniture), or screws — try tightening
- Check leg tips/feet — pads often wear unevenly or fall off
- For cabinets: open door and observe hinge adjustment screws (European hinges have 3 adjustment directions)

## Tools Needed
- Screwdrivers (Phillips and flathead)
- Allen/hex key set (for flat-pack furniture bolts)
- Wood glue (PVA or carpenter's glue)
- Clamps (or tape as substitute)
- Rubber mallet
- Furniture leg levelers (stick-on pads)
- Sandpaper (for drawer)

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. For wobble → check floor first. Place folded paper under each leg to test — if one leg lifts ≥ 3mm, floor is uneven not furniture. Add stick-on felt foot pad to the short leg.
2. Check and tighten all accessible connector bolts. In flat-pack furniture (IKEA etc.) → use Allen key to tighten all cam locks and bolts. One loose connection propagates wobble through whole piece.
3. For loose glue joint → separate the joint if possible. Remove old dried glue with chisel or sandpaper. Apply fresh wood glue, reassemble, and clamp for at least 2 hours. Wipe excess glue before it dries.
4. For joint you can't fully separate → inject wood glue into gap with a toothpick or syringe applicator. Clamp and hold. This works for minor gaps.
5. For chair with loose stretchers (horizontal braces between legs) → these are common failure points. Inject glue, clamp each joint. Let dry overnight before sitting on chair.
6. For sagging shelf → add a center support bracket to underside. Alternatively, add a shelf below the sagging one to carry load from underneath.
7. For crooked cabinet door (European hinges) → most European/cup hinges have 3 adjustment screws: depth (in/out), height (up/down), lateral (left/right). Turn each in small increments (1/4 turn). Close and check alignment after each adjustment.
8. For stiff drawer → remove drawer fully. Rub a candle or wax along the wooden runners (top and bottom edges of drawer sides and wooden tracks). Reinstall.
9. For swollen drawer (humidity) → sand the runner edges slightly (light pass with 120 grit sandpaper). Do not over-sand — better to take off too little and test repeatedly.
10. For cracked leg that hasn't snapped → drill thin pilot holes at both ends of crack (prevents crack from spreading). Apply wood glue into crack, clamp. After drying, reinforce underneath with flat metal corner bracket.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** sitting on or loading a chair/stool with a snapped leg — structural failure risk
- **STOP** if shelf above eye level is heavily loaded and appears to be pulling away from wall — items could fall
- **STOP** if a bookcase or tall shelf unit is not secured to wall — tip-over hazard (especially with children present)
- Do not attempt to repair a broken leg on a high stool or ladder chair — replace it

## Prevention
- Tighten all connector bolts annually on flat-pack furniture — they work loose naturally
- Apply wood glue preventatively to any joint showing early movement — much easier than full re-glue later
- Anchor tall furniture (bookcases, wardrobes, shelving units) to wall studs using L-brackets
- Add felt pads to all furniture feet — reduces floor wobble and protects floors
- Avoid overloading shelves beyond rated capacity — most shelf bowing is permanent
