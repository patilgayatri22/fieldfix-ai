# Bike Chain — Repair Guide

## Common Symptoms
- Chain slips or skips when pedaling hard
- Chain makes clicking or grinding noise
- Chain has fallen off chainring or cassette
- Chain is visibly rusty or stiff
- Shifting is slow or chain won't move to certain gears
- Chain snapped completely
- Chain stretches and sags visibly

## Likely Causes
- Chain skipping/slipping → chain worn (stretched), cassette teeth worn, derailleur limit screw wrong
- Clicking/grinding → dry chain (needs lubrication), stiff link, misaligned derailleur
- Chain fell off → derailleur limit screw out of adjustment, ring damage, extreme cross-chaining
- Rust/stiff chain → chain not cleaned/lubricated, stored wet, old chain
- Poor shifting → cable tension wrong, derailleur hanger bent, cable housing worn
- Chain snapped → extreme force on worn chain, chain link failure, too-small replacement link
- Chain sag → chain is stretched beyond wear limit, needs replacement

## Safe First Checks
- Spin pedals backward by hand and watch chain travel — look for stiff links (link that doesn't flex smoothly)
- Check chain lubrication — chain should not be dry or squeaky
- Check chain wear: place a ruler along chain — 12 links should measure exactly 12 inches (30.48cm). Over 12.1 inches = replace chain
- Check cassette teeth — worn teeth look hooked or shark-finned (normal teeth are symmetrical)
- Check chain has not crossed too many gears (e.g., big-big or small-small combinations — avoid these)

## Tools Needed
- Chain lube (wet or dry depending on conditions)
- Clean rag
- Chain tool (for broken chain repair)
- Replacement master link (carry as spare)
- Ruler or chain wear indicator tool

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. For dropped chain → with bike stopped, use a stick or gloved hand to loop chain back over chainring at front. Avoid using bare hands near greasy/sharp chainring teeth.
2. For stiff link → find the stiff link (it doesn't flex when bent side to side). Apply extra lube to that link. Work it with fingers or flex gently side to side. If chain tool available, use it to relieve lateral pressure on that pin.
3. For dry/noisy chain → clean chain with rag — hold rag around chain and pedal backward. Apply chain lube to each roller (inside of chain) while pedaling backward slowly. Wipe off excess lube from outside — lube attracts dirt.
4. For rusty chain → apply penetrating oil and work it in. If rust is surface only and chain flexes fully → clean and re-lubricate. If links are seized and won't flex → replace chain.
5. For chain skipping under load → check chain wear with ruler. If worn → replace chain. Also check if cassette teeth are worn (shark-finned). Worn cassette + new chain = still skips — may need cassette replacement too.
6. For poor shifting → check cable tension. Turn barrel adjuster (at derailleur or shifter) counterclockwise (half turn at a time) to add tension. Test after each adjustment.
7. For chain won't reach certain gears → check derailleur limit screws (H and L screws on rear derailleur). If chain overshoots or undershoots → adjust H screw (high gear/small cog) or L screw (low gear/large cog) in small increments.
8. For broken chain → use chain tool to remove damaged links. Install replacement master link (quick link) — most modern chains use master links, not pins. Close master link by pulling cranks forward with chain on large chainring under load.
9. After any chain work → shift through all gears in both directions. Pedal at moderate load to verify no skipping.
10. For persistent skip after chain and cassette replacement → check chainring teeth wear. If worn → replace chainring.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if chain falls off while riding — do not attempt to replace while moving, stop safely first
- **STOP** riding on a snapped or severely kinked chain — chain failure at speed is dangerous
- **STOP** if rear derailleur hanger appears severely bent — bent hanger can cause chain to jam in spokes during riding (very dangerous)
- Do not ride with a chain that skips constantly under load — unexpected loss of pedaling resistance causes falls

## Prevention
- Lubricate chain every 100–200km or after wet rides
- Replace chain before it exceeds wear limit (12.1 inches per 12 links) — prevents cassette wear
- Keep drivechain clean — wipe after muddy or wet rides
- Carry a master link (quick link) as field spare
- Avoid cross-chaining (big-big or small-small gear combinations) — causes chain wear
