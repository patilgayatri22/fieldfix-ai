# General Batteries — Repair Guide (AA / AAA / NiMH / Alkaline / Battery Holders)

## Common Symptoms
- Device powers on briefly then dies
- Device won't turn on at all despite fresh batteries
- Batteries drain much faster than expected
- Visible white/blue crystalline residue in battery compartment
- Battery compartment contacts are corroded or blackened
- Device works fine on mains power but not batteries
- New batteries go flat within hours
- Device shows low battery immediately after inserting full batteries

## Likely Causes
- Powers on briefly → batteries nearly depleted, high internal resistance in old batteries
- Won't turn on → batteries inserted wrong polarity, corroded contacts, batteries dead
- Fast drain → parasitic current drain in device, high-load device needs high-drain batteries
- Crystalline residue → alkaline battery leakage (potassium hydroxide) — corrosive
- Corroded contacts → battery leakage, high humidity, long-term storage with batteries installed
- Works on mains not batteries → batteries too weak for peak current demand, contact resistance too high
- New batteries flat fast → counterfeit batteries, high self-discharge NiMH, device always on
- Shows low immediately → device calibrated for alkaline but NiMH used (different voltage curve)

## Safe First Checks
- Check battery polarity markings in compartment — match + and – correctly
- Measure battery voltage with multimeter — alkaline AA full = 1.5V, dead < 1.2V; NiMH full = 1.4V, dead < 1.0V
- Inspect contacts for corrosion or leakage residue
- Check contact springs for tension — should compress and spring back firmly
- For suspected leakage: look for white/blue crust at battery ends or on contacts

## Tools Needed
- Multimeter
- Pencil eraser (contact cleaning)
- Isopropyl alcohol and cotton swab
- White vinegar (neutralize alkaline leakage)
- Sandpaper (80–120 grit) for heavy corrosion

## Step-by-Step Guidance
1. Remove batteries. Measure each battery voltage individually. Replace any below 1.2V (alkaline) or 1.0V (NiMH).
2. Inspect battery compartment. If white/blue residue present → battery has leaked. Use white vinegar to neutralize alkaline leakage (apply with cotton swab, let sit 1 minute, wipe away).
3. Clean contacts with pencil eraser — rub gently to remove oxidation layer. Follow with isopropyl alcohol wipe.
4. For heavily corroded contacts → use fine sandpaper (80 grit) carefully on contact surface. Wipe clean. Apply thin coat of conductive contact grease if available.
5. Check contact spring tension. Press spring with finger — should push back with firm resistance. Bent-flat springs can be carefully bent back with small screwdriver.
6. If spring contact is broken → repair with conductive epoxy or replace contact if spares available. Alternatively, fold a small piece of aluminum foil to bridge the gap temporarily.
7. For device working on mains not batteries → test with fresh premium-brand batteries. Cheap batteries have high internal resistance that causes voltage to collapse under load.
8. For NiMH showing low immediately → device may be calibrated for alkaline (1.5V). NiMH nominal is 1.2V. This is normal behavior in some devices — use alkaline or adjust device's low-battery threshold if configurable.
9. For fast drain → measure device's standby current (multimeter in series, in µA/mA mode). High standby current indicates device not entering sleep mode properly.
10. Always replace all batteries in a device at the same time — mixing old and new causes old batteries to be drained by new ones.

## Stop Conditions
- **STOP** if battery leakage residue contacts skin — rinse immediately with water; alkaline leakage (KOH) is caustic
- **STOP** if any battery is hot, swollen, or deformed — remove carefully, do not attempt to charge
- **STOP** if attempting to charge primary (non-rechargeable) alkaline batteries — fire risk
- Never mix battery types or mix old and new in same device

## Prevention
- Remove batteries if device will be stored for more than 2 months
- Use NiMH rechargeable batteries for high-drain devices (digital cameras, flashlights)
- Clean contacts annually even without leakage to prevent oxidation buildup
- Buy batteries from reputable retailers — counterfeit batteries are common
- Label battery compartments with installation date to track age
